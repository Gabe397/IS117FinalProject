Bob Taylor

The Man Who Had An Idea

Bob Taylor was the man who started the team whose sole mission was to connect computers so that they can communicate with each other. Taylor’s main problem was that researchers across the country demanded computers, additionally researchers were also having trouble communicating with each other so there were lots of duplicate research going on wasting company resources. Taylor wanted a way so that there could be a system of electronic machines that were connected so that researchers across the country could share resources and knowledge with each other more efficiently. So in 1966, Taylor constructed a team lead by Lawrence Roberts to create this network of computers to solve this communication problem that faced ARPA.

List of Accomplishments

  • Director of ARPA's IPTO
  • Draper Prize
  • National Medal of Technology and Innovation
  • Program Manager for NASA
  • ACM System Award
  • Computer History Museum Fellow
  • ACM Fellow
