History of ARPA

The Birth of the Internet

ARPA was created in 1958 by President Dwight D. Eisenhower as a response to the Sputnik launch by the Soviets. Its purpose was to advance American technological capabilities by pairing researchers, industry and government bodies together. ARPAnet would be the byproduct of ARPA when men such as Donald Davies, Bob Taylor, Lawrence Roberts, Leonard Kleinrock, Paul Baran and many others successfully connected computers to communicate with each other. ARPAnet would become the predecessor to the modern day internet.
